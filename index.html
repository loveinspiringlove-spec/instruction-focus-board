Focus Board

How to use your practice

The Focus Board is your visual practice of pointing — of placing your attention deliberately on the things you are calling into your life, and letting yourself feel them as already on their way.

The practice
1
Name your board
Tap the soft italic line beneath the title and give this board a name — a season, an intention, a feeling. "Summer 2026" or "The life I'm stepping into." This anchors it in time and energy.
2
Plant your desires
Tap any empty tile to open the planting panel. Write what you want in present, positive language. Short phrases land best — "New car", "Hawaii trip", "Financial ease." Twelve tiles, twelve desires.
3
Return and focus
Open your board daily. Tap any filled tile to highlight it with a golden border — your signal to yourself that you are actively pointing toward this. Tap again to release it. Multiple tiles can be in focus at once.
4
Crown today's desire
Double-tap a filled tile to anoint it as your today's focus — a ✦ appears in the corner. Only one tile holds this distinction at a time. This is the desire you are living into today, the one you are finding evidence for right now.
5
Save & share
When your board is full and alive, tap Save & Share to create a beautiful image of your board — perfect to save to your camera roll, set as a wallpaper, or share with the community.

✦   Your board saves automatically on your device. Every desire you plant stays exactly as you left it the next time you open it.   ✦

A note on the energy

This practice is Abraham-Hicks in its purest form: pointing. You are not using willpower to manifest. You are simply noticing that this desire exists, that it is real, and that it is already on its way. The board is not a to-do list. It is a feeling place — somewhere you return to when you want to remember what you are flowing your energy toward.

Visit it in the morning before the day takes over. Visit it when something wonderful happens and you want to feel even more of that. Let it be alive and changeable — swap out what's been received, add new desires as they arise. This is a living practice.
